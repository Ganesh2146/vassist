What Exactly is the Dark Web?
🌐
DEFINITION
A portion of the internet not indexed by search engines, accessible only via Tor Browser which routes traffic through layered encryption — making you anonymous.
🧅
.ONION DOMAINS
Sites end in .onion — 56-character cryptographic hashes with no DNS registration and no fixed IP. Invisible to regular browsers entirely.
🛡️
ORIGIN
Built by the U.S. Naval Research Lab in the 1990s for secure military communications. Later open-sourced for journalists in censored regions.
⚠️
DARK ≠ DEEP WEB
The Deep Web = private but legal (your Gmail, bank). The Dark Web = intentionally hidden, requiring anonymous routing tools. Completely different things.
History of the Dark Web
1990s
U.S. Navy Creates Tor
Naval Research Lab develops onion routing for secure military communications over the public internet.
2002
Tor Project Released Publicly
Tor Browser released as open-source. Journalists, activists, and privacy advocates in censored countries gain anonymous internet access.
2011
Silk Road Launches
First major dark web marketplace for illegal drugs — operated by "Dread Pirate Roberts." Accepted only Bitcoin. Changed the dark web forever.
2013
FBI Takes Down Silk Road
FBI seizes $1 billion in Bitcoin and arrests Ross Ulbricht. Proves no one is truly anonymous — even Tor has limits.
2024+
Modern Era: AI Meets the Dark Web
AI-generated malware, deepfakes, and data trading surge. Global agencies use AI to fight back. The arms race continues.

HOW ANONYMITY IS ACHIEVED
01
Multi-Layer EncryptionYour request gets wrapped in 3 layers of encryption — like an onion.
02
Entry NodeKnows your IP, not your destination. Strips outer layer, passes on.
03
Middle RelayKnows only previous & next hop. Full path is impossible to reconstruct.
04
Exit NodeStrips final layer, delivers request. Site sees exit IP — never yours.
05
.onion RoutingFor dark web sites, data never leaves Tor. End-to-end invisible.
